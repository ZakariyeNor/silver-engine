Universal facial expressions

Universal facial expressions are emotional expressions that are understood and recognized by people across different cultures. These expressions include happiness, sadness, anger, fear, surprise, and disgust.

The two major categories of body language

Body language can be broken down into 2 major categories—positive or open body language and negative or closed body language.

open body language examples

the eyebrow flash

When someone does an eyebrow flash, you’ll typically see their eyebrows raise slightly for less than ⅕ of a second. What it Means: The eyebrow raise is a great sign of interest. People tend to use the eyebrow flash in 3 main ways: The eyebrow flash can show interest professionally, as when giving approval, agreeing to something, thanking someone, or seeking confirmation. It’s used as a nonverbal “yes” during conversation. The eyebrow flash can also show interest romantically. Or the eyebrow flash can show interest socially, as when 2 people recognize each other. It signals to the other person that you are happy to see them. Whenever we use the eyebrow flash, we call attention to our face. Teachers and speakers often use it as a way to say, “Listen to this!” or “Look at me!”

the equel handshake

The equal handshake is a common form of greeting and a way to establish rapport between two individuals. It involves each person extending their hand towards the other, gripping firmly, making eye contact, and shaking hands in a balanced manner. This type of handshake signifies equality and mutual respect between the individuals involved. An equal handshake is typically open and positive body language, as it conveys confidence, trust, and a willingness to engage with the other person. It sets a tone of equality and partnership, promoting a sense of collaboration and cooperation. By shaking hands in this way, individuals are demonstrating their respect for each other and their willingness to engage in a respectful interaction. Overall, the equal handshake is a simple yet powerful gesture that can help to establish a positive connection between people. It is a universal sign of goodwill and can help to create a sense of trust and unity in a variety of social and professional situations.

closed body language examples

Physical Retreat

Physical retreat in body language can often be a sign of negativity or feeling closed off. This can manifest in various ways, such as crossing arms, avoiding eye contact, or turning away from others. The act of physically retreating can signal discomfort, defensiveness, or a desire to distance oneself from a situation or individual. Crossing arms, for example, creates a barrier between oneself and others, signaling a closed-off attitude. Avoiding eye contact can indicate unease or disinterest in engaging with others. Turning away can suggest a desire to escape or avoid confrontation. Overall, physical retreat in body language major of negativity or closed off can create a barrier to effective communication and connection with others. It can be important to be aware of these signals in both oneself and others to address any underlying issues and foster a more open and positive environment for interaction.

Pocketed hands

Keeping hands in pockets may indicate disinterest or discomfort in revealing one’s thoughts and feelings. What it Means: Having your hands in your pockets during a conversation generally signals a reserved or closed-off attitude. It might mean you’re uncomfortable, disinterested, or unwilling to engage fully with the other person. This gesture often hampers open communication and can make you appear unapproachable. The Science: Psychological research21 suggests that hand gestures contribute significantly to communication. Therefore, pocketed hands limit this expressive capability, often leading to misinterpretation or a lack of connection during interactions. How to Use it: If you notice yourself resorting to this stance, it may be helpful to ask yourself: “Am I nervous, uncomfortable, or disengaged?” Likewise, if you observe someone else with pocketed hands, it might be a sign to approach the situation with greater sensitivity.
